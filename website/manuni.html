Our Stadiums

Past, Present, Future.

Old Trafford
1910 - 1966

Old Trafford has been Manchester United’s home since 1910. Located in Greater Manchester, it is one of the most iconic football stadiums in the world, with a capacity of over 74,000 fans.

It has hosted numerous memorable matches, including the 1966 World Cup final and the UEFA Champions League finals.

Modern Old Trafford
1966 - now

Modern Old Trafford has seen significant upgrades, including improved seating, enhanced facilities, and a focus on sustainability. The stadium now accommodates around 76,000 fans and features state-of-the-art technology.

It remains a fortress for Manchester United, hosting countless memorable matches and events.

Redevelopment Plans

Manchester United plans to renovate Old Trafford to enhance its capacity and facilities by 2026. The project includes a new hotel, covered fan zone, and entertainment areas, creating a year-round destination for football and events.

The expansion reflects the club’s ambition to remain one of the top global sports organizations.
