Our Stadiums

Past, Present, Future.

Estadio Chamartín
1923 - 1947

Estadio Chamartín was Real Madrid’s home from 1923 to 1947. Located in the Chamartín district of Madrid, it was famous for its passionate atmosphere and once held over 75,000 fans.

It hosted league matches, Copa del Rey finals, and international games.

Santiago Bernabéu
1947 - now

Estadio Santiago Bernabéu, opened in 1947, is now Real Madrid’s home. It holds around 81,044 spectators and features modern facilities, advanced lighting, and a full training complex known as the Real Madrid City.

It symbolizes the club’s rise in world football and commitment to innovation and sustainability.

New Bernabéu

Real Madrid plans to renovate the Santiago Bernabéu Stadium to enhance its capacity and facilities by 2026. The project includes a new hotel, covered fan zone, and entertainment areas, creating a year-round destination for football and events.

The expansion reflects the club’s ambition to remain one of the top global sports organizations.
