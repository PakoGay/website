Our Stadiums

Past, Present, Future.

MAINE ROAD
1923 - 2003

Maine Road was Manchester City’s home from 1923 to 2003. Located in Moss Side, it was famous for passionate atmosphere and once held over 84,000 fans.

It hosted league matches, FA Cup semifinals, and international games. In 2003, the club left Maine Road for a new modern stadium, marking the end of an era.

ETIHAD
2003 - now

The Etihad Stadium, opened in 2003, is now Manchester City’s home. It holds around 53,000 spectators and features modern facilities, advanced lighting, and a full training complex known as the Etihad Campus.

It symbolizes the club’s rise in world football and commitment to innovation and sustainability.

Etihad Expansion

Manchester City plans to expand the Etihad Stadium to over 60,000 seats by 2026. The project includes a new hotel, covered fan zone, and entertainment areas, creating a year-round destination for football and events.

The expansion reflects the club’s ambition to remain one of the top global sports organizations.
